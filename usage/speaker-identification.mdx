---
title: Speaker identification
description: Label and rename individual speakers in a completed transcription.
---

Speaker identification analyses a completed transcription and marks each segment with the speaker who said it. You can then preview, rename, and merge speaker entries before saving.

<Warning>
  Speaker identification is not available on Intel-based Macs. The underlying diarization library does not support that platform.
</Warning>

## Identify speakers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the transcription">
    In the main window, double-click a completed transcription (or select it and click the **⤢** icon) to open the [transcription viewer](/usage/transcription-viewer).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Identify Speakers">
    Click the **Identify Speakers** button in the toolbar. Buzz analyses the source audio file and assigns speaker labels (e.g. **Speaker 1**, **Speaker 2**) to each segment.

    <Note>
      The source audio file must still be present on your system for speaker identification to work.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the results">
    Each segment in the transcript now shows a speaker label. Segments from the same speaker are grouped together.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Preview a speaker

To verify that a label matches the right person, click the **preview** button next to a speaker label. Buzz plays a 10-second sample from a random sentence attributed to that speaker.

## Rename speaker labels

Click a speaker label and type a new name to replace the automatically generated identifier (e.g. change **Speaker 1** to **Alice**). Renaming a label updates it across all segments attributed to that speaker.

## Merge consecutive sentences

Check **Merge speaker sentences** before saving. When enabled, Buzz combines consecutive segments from the same speaker into a single segment, reducing the total number of rows in the transcript and making it easier to read.

## Save

Click **Save** to apply your speaker labels (and any merges) to the transcription. The segment table updates immediately.
